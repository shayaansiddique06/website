Correct isn’t the same as measurable.

Reinforcement learning from verifiable rewards was a breakthrough for math and code: instead of a learned judge that can be fooled, a deterministic checker decides whether an answer is right. But it didn’t solve everything. Models still game what the checker fails to enforce: a proof can compile cleanly while proving the wrong theorem. Machine-checking alone isn’t enough. To actually measure reasoning, the corpus has to be organized along the one axis the checker ignores: faithfulness, whether a formal proof captures what the original theorem meant.

  • Compiles ≠ faithful A Lean proof can typecheck while proving the wrong statement.
  • Faithfulness is its own axis Something you measure, not assume.
  • Strength-structured lattice Proofs organized by logical strength, not just pass/fail.
