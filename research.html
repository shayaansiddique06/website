What the corpus measures.

Specification gaming on verified math

Verifiable rewards make one kind of exploit concrete: the gaps in the verifier itself. The corpus is built to find and label the cases where a formal proof satisfies the checker but violates intent.

Weakening attractors in self-improving reasoners

When a model trains on math it verifies itself, it can drift toward statements that are weaker but easier to prove. The strength-structured lattice makes that drift measurable.

The strength-structured lattice

We organize proofs not as pass/fail but in a lattice ordered by logical strength, so anyone using the corpus can measure how strong a model’s verified reasoning actually is, not just whether it passed.
